## 1. Headline finding

The structural validator scored this thesis **90/100 PASS** and flagged
0 missing fields. Manual fact-check finds:

- **4 numerical claims VERIFIED** within tolerance against ingested data
- **2 ANOMALIES the structural validator does not catch:**
  1. `step_1_catalyst.catalyst_date_or_window = "2025-11-12"` is 6 months
     in the past relative to thesis generation date 2026-05-05
  2. `step_2_mechanism` claims "current ~17x forward P/E" — yahoo's actual
     `pe_forward = 12.16x`; the 17.06x is the **trailing** P/E. Thesis
     mislabels trailing as forward.
- **4 claims UNVERIFIABLE** from currently-ingested sources (quarterly
  granularity gaps, segment-level gaps, non-IFRS reconciliation)

Both anomalies are **factual-correctness issues a structural compliance
validator cannot detect** — they require cross-check against external
ground truth. This is exactly the gap §8 of the audit re-run flagged.

### 2.2 ANOMALIES — factual issues structural validator did not catch (n=2)

#### A1 — Temporal: catalyst date in the past

**Claim:** `step_1_catalyst.catalyst_date_or_window` = `"2025-11-12 (Q3 2025 earnings, expected window)"`

**Reality:** Today is 2026-05-05. The Q3 2025 earnings release was 6
months ago. Tencent has since released Q4 2025 / FY25 results
(2026-03 reporting per typical calendar), and we have FY25 figures
in `fin_700_HK.json` (2025-12-31).

**Structural validator status:** PASSED. The check
`step1Present && hasDateOrWindow(...) && !isBoilerplate(...)`
confirmed the field is present, parses as a date, and isn't generic
boilerplate. It does NOT check whether the date is **in the future**
relative to thesis generation.

**Why this matters:**
The thesis is structurally framed as forward-looking ("re-rating
toward 19-20x as the AI/Hunyuan narrative layer adds multiple
expansion") but anchored on a catalyst that has already occurred.
Either: (a) the model is hallucinating temporal context, (b) the model
is doing retroactive analysis presented in prospective voice, or
(c) the model treats the catalyst as the framing reference even after
it fired. None of those are wrong by themselves, but the validator
gives this thesis a clean "phase_1_market_belief duration_estimate
= until 2025-11-12" timeline that is physically inconsistent with
the present.

**What investment-quality validation requires here:**
Add a temporal validity check to `validateThesisQuality`: for
catalyst dates, require `parsed_date > thesis_generation_date`.
Estimate: ~30 min KR. Closes one structurally invisible failure mode.

#### A2 — Definition: "forward P/E" is actually trailing P/E

**Claim:** `step_2_mechanism[5]` chain step says: "...a re-rating from
**current ~17x forward P/E** toward 19-20x..."

**Reality:**
- yahoo `pe_trailing = 17.063x`
- yahoo `pe_forward = 12.161x`
- The thesis's "17x" matches **trailing**, not forward.
- Forward P/E is ~28% lower (12.16x), implying yahoo's consensus FY26
  EPS estimate is materially higher than FY25 actual (TTM EPS ≈ HKD
  26.21, implied yahoo forward EPS ≈ HKD 38.9, +48% YoY — possibly
  yahoo using outlier high estimate).

**Structural validator status:** PASSED. There is no check that links
multiplier claims to ingested live fundamentals.

**Why this matters:**
The thesis builds its "19-20x re-rating → +30-40% upside" math on
the multiplier expanding from "17x". If the multiplier is actually
already 12.16x forward, the rerating math is even more dramatic
(12 → 19 = +58%, far above thesis's +30-40%) — OR if the thesis is
implicitly using trailing/FY25 P/E, then the multiple claim is
honest but the label "forward" is wrong. Either way, the math is
on shifty footing without a cross-check.

**What investment-quality validation requires here:**
Cross-check thesis multiplier claims against
`market_data.yahoo[ticker].fundamentals.pe_trailing /
pe_forward / ev_ebitda / ps_ratio`. When thesis says "17x forward",
diff against `pe_forward`; flag if Δ > 10%.

## 3. Systemic gaps surfaced by this pilot

What the pilot reveals about real fact-check infra needs (not what
to build, but what's missing):

1. **No temporal validity check.** Easy add to existing structural
   validator. ~30 min KR. Eliminates A1-class anomaly. Trivial cost.
2. **No cross-check of multiplier / ratio claims vs live data.**
   Eliminates A2-class anomaly. Requires ~150-300 LOC Python script
   that picks specific claim-path patterns ("X forward P/E", "Y EV/EBITDA")
   and diffs against `market_data.yahoo[ticker].fundamentals`. ~2-3 hour KR.
3. **Quarterly granularity gap for HK tickers.** Many thesis claims
   live at the quarterly level. AKShare provides quarterly for A-shares;
   HK tickers we only have annual via yfinance. To verify thesis's
   Q2/Q3-specific claims, would need HKEx/AAStocks/EastMoney HK
   quarterly fetcher. Not pilot-blocking but limits coverage to ~30%
   of typical thesis claims for HK.
4. **Segment-level disclosure gap.** Thesis routinely makes claims at
   segment level ("domestic games", "marketing services", "fintech").
   Our data doesn't have segment breakdown for HK. Cninfo PDF parsing
   for A-share, HKEx for HK — both real engineering investments.
5. **Non-IFRS / adjusted reconciliation not ingested.** Tencent reports
   non-IFRS NPAT separately from GAAP. We carry GAAP via yfinance.
   Reconciliation requires parsing earnings releases.

Items 1+2 are **today-doable** with small KRs.
Items 3-5 are **medium-investment data-source projects** matching the
"Bridge 2 — 数据源全面接入" tier in STATUS.md §2.

## 4. What this pilot does NOT prove

Necessary discipline (matches `lesson_oversell_discipline_2026-05-05`):

- **NOT** "thesis is broken" — 4 of 6 checkable claims verified.
- **NOT** "structural validator is useless" — it correctly enforced
  schema, mechanism chain length, sizing curve format, etc. Those are
  necessary preconditions for fact-check to even be possible.
- **NOT** "fact-check methodology generalizes" — n=1 pilot. Need ≥3 to
  see if patterns hold.
- **NOT** investment-quality validation by itself. Even if all
  numbers check, the thesis still requires:
  - Mechanism causal chain to actually be sound (semantic, not numerical)
  - Variant view to be truly contrarian (not rephrased consensus)
  - Falsification conditions to be observable (we have observable
    `wrongIf` metrics — that's separate verification work)
  - wrongIf time-series tracking to confirm thesis ground truth at
    horizon (Q3-Q4 outcomes)

This pilot adds **fact-check** as one verification layer. The full
stack still requires Bridge 8 backtest + Franky expert review.

## 5. Reproducibility checklist

Anyone running this pilot should be able to reproduce:

```bash
# 1. Capture thesis (costs ~1 Opus 4.7 call, 20K tokens, ~$0.96)
curl -X POST https://equity-research-ten.vercel.app/api/research \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"ticker":"700.HK","company":"Tencent","direction":"NEUTRAL","context":"factcheck pilot"}' \
  -o thesis_700_HK.json

# 2. Confirm validator says PASS (or note severity)
python3 -c "import json; d=json.load(open('thesis_700_HK.json'))['data']['_quality']; print(d['severity'], d['score'])"

# 3. Diff numerical claims against fin_*.json + market_data.json
#    (manual for now; future: scripts/thesis_factcheck.py)
```

Note: each thesis call is non-deterministic (Claude sampling). Repeated
runs may produce different exact wording, different catalyst dates,
different multiplier framings. **A claim verified once may not verify
on the next call.** This itself is a finding that argues for
deterministic seeding or post-hoc capture of the canonical thesis.

   `validateThesisQuality` — `step_1_catalyst.catalyst_date_or_window`
